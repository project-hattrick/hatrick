View

How masks work: each pixel gets a soft weight per band from hue-distance × saturation-gate. White (desaturated) and near-black (below dark cutoff) fall out of every hue mask automatically, so they never get miscolored — that's what killed the old faults. Methods: Hue keeps the source shading/texture and just re-tints (closest to the clean hue-shift; best for chromatic→chromatic like navy→red); Colorize sets hue+sat, keeps source lightness; Gradient maps band luminance onto a shadow→base→highlight ramp (great for big luminance jumps, navy→yellow); Multiply tints a level-stretched greyscale; Hybrid ★ = Hue for shirt/socks + Gradient for the white band (needed when shorts change to a real color). Turn on Region split for striped kits (Brazil) so shirt whites don't bleed into shorts color.
