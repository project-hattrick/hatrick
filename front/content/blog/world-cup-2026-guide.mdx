## The biggest World Cup ever

The 2026 World Cup is the first to feature 48 teams, up from 32. It is co-hosted by three nations — the United States, Canada and Mexico — across sixteen host cities. The expansion pushes the tournament from 64 matches to 104, making it the longest and largest men's World Cup in history.

For fans, more teams and more matches mean more football to follow than any previous edition. It also means more first-time qualifiers, more time zones, and a schedule dense enough that keeping track of everything by hand is genuinely hard. That is the gap Hatrick is built to close.

## How the new format works

The 48 teams are split into twelve groups of four. Each team plays three group-stage matches. This is the part that changed most from previous tournaments:

- **The top two from each group advance** — that is 24 teams.
- **The eight best third-placed teams also advance** — bringing the knockout field to 32.
- **The knockout stage** runs Round of 32 → Round of 16 → Quarter-finals → Semi-finals → Final, all single-elimination.

The move to a 32-team knockout bracket is new. It means a group-stage slip no longer ends your tournament automatically — a strong third-place finish can still see you through, which keeps more teams alive deeper into the competition.

## The shape of the schedule

The group stage front-loads the tournament with a dense run of matches, often several per day across overlapping time zones. Once the knockout rounds begin, the pace slows to a smaller number of higher-stakes fixtures.

Because the hosts span three countries and multiple time zones, kickoff times are spread widely across the day. A single afternoon can hold a match in Mexico, another on the US east coast, and a third on the west coast — each with its own local kickoff. Following all of it in real time is the challenge, and it is exactly what the tournament's scale is built around.

## How to follow every match live

This is where Hatrick's two modes come in.

**Live Mode** gives you a real-time 2D view of every match, driven by the TxLINE feed. Goals, cards, substitutions and corners animate the moment they are confirmed, and live odds shift with the score. You do not need to have the broadcast open — the match view and the event feed keep you current on their own.

**Fantasy Mode** turns the tournament into a season-long build. You draft a squad of real World Cup players, and their attribute scores move with their actual performances. A striker on a scoring run gets sharper; a goalkeeper shipping goals gets weaker. The tournament's real drama feeds directly into your squad.

Both modes share one profile, one wallet and one design. You can watch a match in Live Mode, notice a player performing well, and know their Fantasy attributes will reflect it within minutes.

## Getting ready

- **Learn the groups early.** With twelve groups and eight third-place spots up for grabs, group-stage maths matters more than ever. Small results swing who advances.
- **Track players, not just teams.** In Fantasy Mode, individual form is what moves your squad. Watch who is starting and who is scoring.
- **Use Live Mode for the overlap.** When three matches run at once, the real-time view and event feed let you follow all of them without three broadcasts open.
