## What odds actually tell you

Odds are a price. They tell you two things at once: how much a correct prediction returns, and how likely the market thinks that outcome is. On Hat-trick, the odds you see in Live Mode are real-time — they move with the match as it happens, driven by the TxLINE feed.

Once you can read a price, the whole board stops looking like random numbers and starts looking like a live map of what the market believes.

## Decimal odds in one minute

Hat-trick shows decimal odds. The number is the total return per unit staked, including your stake back.

- **Odds of 2.00** — a correct prediction doubles your stake. Stake 10, get 20 back (10 profit).
- **Odds of 1.50** — a strong favourite. Stake 10, get 15 back (5 profit).
- **Odds of 4.00** — an underdog. Stake 10, get 40 back (30 profit).

The lower the number, the more likely the market thinks the outcome is — and the less it pays. The higher the number, the less likely, and the more it pays.

## Turning odds into probability

Decimal odds convert to an implied probability with one small sum: divide 1 by the odds.

- **2.00** → 1 ÷ 2.00 = **50%**
- **1.50** → 1 ÷ 1.50 = **67%**
- **4.00** → 1 ÷ 4.00 = **25%**

This is the single most useful habit you can build. When you look at a price, translate it into a percentage in your head. If the market says a team is 67% to win and you think they are closer to 50%, the two of you disagree — and that disagreement is the whole point of a prediction.

## Why live odds move

In Live Mode, prices are not fixed. They react to the match in real time:

- **A goal** swings the odds hard toward the scoring team the instant TxLINE confirms it.
- **A red card** shifts the balance toward the team with eleven players on the pitch.
- **Time running down** compresses the odds — with fewer minutes left, outcomes get more certain and prices tighten.

Because Hat-trick uses the confirmed TxLINE feed, the price you see reflects verified events, not rumour. When the board moves, something real happened on the pitch.

## The three common markets

Most Live Mode matches expose the same core markets:

- **1X2** — home win, draw, or away win. The classic three-way market.
- **Over/Under** — will the total number of goals be over or under a set line, usually 2.5. This is about how open the game is, not who wins.
- **In-play props** — shorter-lived markets that open and settle during the match, like the next event or a specific outcome.

Each market has its own odds, and each reacts differently to the same event. A goal that helps the favourite in the 1X2 market also pushes the Over line closer — one event, two price moves.

## Reading the board like a pro

- **Always convert to probability.** A price only means something once it is a percentage in your head.
- **Watch the move, not just the number.** A team drifting from 1.80 to 2.20 is telling you the market is losing faith — that trend is information.
- **Remember the margin.** Add up the implied probabilities of all outcomes and you will get slightly more than 100%. That overround is the built-in margin; it is normal, and it means the true probabilities are a touch lower than the raw prices suggest.
- **Let the feed do the work.** In Live Mode you do not have to refresh anything. The odds update themselves as the match unfolds, so you can focus on reading the move instead of chasing the number.
